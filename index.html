Merry Christmas!


Christmas is a Christian festival that celebrates the birth of Jesus Christ, who Christians believe was the son of God. For most people, it takes place every year on 25 December – the day that the Roman Catholic Church chose to mark Jesus’ birthday. But, in fact, no one actually knows the exact date Jesus was born!


The name ‘Christmas’ comes from the old English phrase Cristes maesse, which means ‘Christ’s mass’. But what about ‘Xmas’? Lots of people think this is just a modern-day abbreviation – but it actually dates back to the 16th century! The ‘X’ is said to represent the Greek letter ‘Chi’– the first letter in the Greek word for Christ, Χριστός (pronounced ‘Christos’).

The Christmas story is a story of a family that connects heaven and earth. Each member of Jesus's earthly family—Mary, Joseph, and Jesus—stands as supernal examples of God's Christmas gift to all mankind. The Christmas story should spiritually motivate us to emulate the attributes of this holy family.
